I. THE INITIAL STAGNATION

The morning of October 12th was punctuated not by noise, but by an unsettling, hollow quiet. The transit veins of the city—once pulsating with the rhythmic clatter of local trains and the baritone rumble of red double-decker buses—had been severed by administrative decree.

Within hours, the first "Event Nodes" began to manifest. What we now classify as Structural Drift began in the northern outskirts. Documented reports from sector marshals indicated a 40% drop in pedestrian movement within the first six hours of the order's enactment.

II. THE CATALYTIC RESPONSE

By sundown, the stagnation had birthed resistance. The Causality Tree reveals that the 'Civic Response' node was triggered not by political affiliation, but by basic logistical necessity. The transit halt prevented 1.2 million daily workers from reaching the commercial core, effectively paralyzing the tax base.
